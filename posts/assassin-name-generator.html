The Meaning and Derivation of Assassin Names

Understanding the etymology and symbolism behind assassin names adds depth to character creation. Many assassin names draw from specific linguistic roots, cultural references, and symbolic meanings:

  • Shadow and Darkness: Assassin names often incorporate elements of shadow, darkness, and stealth. Names like 'Shadow', 'Night', 'Void', and 'Shade' reflect their work in the shadows.
  • Weapons and Tools: Names frequently reference weapons and tools of the trade: 'Blade', 'Dagger', 'Arrow', 'Silk' (for garrotes). These names suggest skill and precision.
  • Animals of the Night: Many assassin names reference nocturnal animals: 'Raven', 'Crow', 'Owl', 'Bat', 'Wolf'. These animals symbolize stealth, intelligence, and deadly precision.
  • Death and Mortality: Some assassin names reference death, mortality, and the finality of their work: 'Reaper', 'Grim', 'Mort', 'Grave'. These names emphasize the serious nature of their profession.
  • Cultural Origins: Assassin names often reflect their cultural origins: Italian names (Ezio, Corvo), Arabic names (Altair), Japanese names (for ninja), etc. This adds authenticity and depth.
  • Epithets and Titles: Assassins often have epithets that reflect their methods or reputation: 'the Silent', 'the Shadow', 'the Ghost', 'the Faceless'. These add mystery and establish their legend.

When creating assassin names, consider what each element means and how it contributes to the assassin's identity. A name like 'Corvo Attano' (raven) immediately suggests stealth and darkness, while 'Altair' (the flying one) suggests both freedom and deadly precision.
